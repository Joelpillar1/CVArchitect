# Resume Bullet Points Guide - 100 Examples

## Persona: 15-Year Google Recruiter
Title: "I’ve Spent 15 Years Recruiting at Google: Here are 100+ Resume Bullet Points That Actually Get You Hired"

## Why Verbs Matter
Bullet points are the heartbeat of your resume. As a recruiter at Google, I scan thousands of resumes. I don't read "duties." I look for "impact." 
Verbs are the engine of that impact. "Responsible for" is passive. "Spearheaded" is active. 
Using high-impact verbs changes the tone from "I was there" to "I made it happen."

## The APR Formula (Action + Project + Result)
Every bullet point should follow this:
- **Action Verb**: What did you do? (e.g., Developed, Optimized)
- **Project/Context**: What was the task or scope? (e.g., 3-month marketing campaign)
- **Result**: What was the outcome? (e.g., Increased ROI by 15%)
